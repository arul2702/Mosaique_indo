However, there are many barriers to hiring highly skilled human resources. Both companies accepting foreigners and human resources going to Japan need to understand and deal with language issues, differences in culture, and work styles.


Language problem

English is a universal language, but regional dialects in India and various languages across Europe and America create communication challenges. Rural India faces language barriers, hindering access to essential services. Understanding Indian languages and cultural subtleties presents challenges for Europe and America, requiring substantial resources for translation and localization.


Skill problem

India's high youth unemployment is attributed to graduates' insufficient job-ready skills, rural-urban disparities, and limited awareness of emerging job markets. Comprehensive reforms like updating curricula, improving vocational training, and bridging skill gaps are essential for empowering the workforce and promoting inclusive economic growth.


Culture difference

India's diverse cultural landscape, influenced by traditions, religions, and languages, influences social norms and customs. Family relationships are strong, while individualism and success are valued more in Europe and America. Social institutions value equality and meritocracy, while cuisine, clothing, and festivals reflect different histories and climates.


Lack of understanding towards Indians

Cultural differences and misinterpretations are frequently the source of misunderstandings about Indians. Language hurdles, various cultures, and historical circumstances all lead to misconceptions. Stereotypes and biases further hinder true comprehension. Such beliefs can impair productive communication, commercial transactions, and interpersonal relationships.


Concept of time and delivery date

Cultural differences in time and delivery dates between India and Europe/America can lead to misconceptions in cross-cultural commercial dealings. In India, deadlines are flexible, focusing on relationships and responding to unexpected events, while in Europe/America, precise deadlines are prioritized. Effective communication and cultural understanding are crucial for successful collaboration.

EOR (Employer of Record) business

There are various methods to expand your business internationally, such as establishing a representative office, branch office, or local subsidiary. However, each approach comes with its own set of costs and risks. Furthermore, the process of starting a business, like setting up a local corporation or opening a representative office, typically takes about a year, posing challenges for rapid international expansion. Nevertheless, by leveraging a suite of employment agency services known as "EOR" (Employer of Record), you can avoid back-office expenses like tax filings and personnel procedures, eliminating the need to establish a local corporation. The timeframe for launching a business varies case by case, but it's possible to initiate operations in as little as a week. Additionally, there's no requirement for corporate dissolution upon withdrawal.
